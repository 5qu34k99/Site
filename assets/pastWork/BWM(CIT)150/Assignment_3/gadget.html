Investigating and Checking Hyperlinks


How much does a link to Google Gadget slow down page rendering?

The page took about twice as long to render.

Such considerations are important when creating a hyperlink to a remote resource that automatically renders each time your page opens in a client’s browser.

How does the added gadget appear on the page?

It looks OK. I would prefer it to be more stylized. There is a lot of unused white space that is distracting. The content would look better centered in the white space.

Does it enhance your page or make your page look cluttered?

Both. The style aesthetic of the gadget makes my page look cluttered, but the photos are a pleasant addition. The advertisement on the gadget adds to the clutter aspect of it.

Now that you have added an hyperlink to a remote resource, what happens if the remote resource changes its URL or otherwise becomes unavailable?

The gadget would no longer work. The link would not be valid, a bad link, and my page would be ranked lower by search engines as a result.

Remember that you do not have any control over what Google or Google’s partners do with these applications.

Use multiple browsers to view your page (including a text-based browser).

How does the Google Gadget appear in all browsers?

In Firefox, Chrome, Opera, and Safari the gadget appears uniform. In a text browser the alternate (text) description of the gadget is displayed.

Remember that a bad hyperlink will cause a page to be ranked lower by a search engine such as Google, Yahoo! or MSN. Open a Web browser and research the following applications to help you reduce the likihood of bad hyperlinks:

  • Linklint – http://www.linklint.org
  • Link Controller – http://freshmeat.net/projects/linkcont/
  • Checkbot – http://degraaff.org/checkbot/
